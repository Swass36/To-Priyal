Dear Chitubhi,

You’ve always been a hero to me.
Not the kind with capes or medals, but the kind who walks through storms just to keep the people they love from feeling the rain.
I’ve watched you fight for others, stand tall in rooms that tried to shrink you, and hold yourself together when everything around you was falling apart.

But I also saw the shattering.
The quiet breaking no one else noticed. The pieces of you that kept getting smaller so others could breathe easier. And I wish—God, I wish—I could’ve held you tighter, earlier. I wish I could’ve told you how proud I was of the way you loved so fiercely, even when it hurt.

I know there are choices you made that you regret. You carry them like silent ghosts behind your smile.
But I realized something from my own experiences-
“Every choice I ever make will require me to mourn the loss of every choice I can no longer make as a result.”
And that’s such a heavy thing to live with. But I want you to know: I see you. All of you. And I don’t need you to be perfect. I just need you to breathe.

So now, whatever strength I have — every drop — I’m giving it to you.
You held me up when I couldn’t walk through the dark, and now it’s my turn. I’ll carry the weight with you, for you, always.
You were my hero even when you felt like a mess.
You are my hero, even now.

And I’ll be yours, if you ever need one.

Love,
